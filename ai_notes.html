Unit 1: Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

What is AI?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the ability of machines to mimic human intelligence, including learning, problem-solving, and decision-making.

Why Artificial Intelligence?

  • Solves real-world problems like healthcare, marketing, and automation.
  • Powers virtual assistants like Siri and Google Assistant.
  • Enables robots to work in hazardous environments.

Subfields of AI

  • Machine Learning: Algorithms that allow computers to learn from data.
  • Computer Vision: Enables machines to interpret visual information.
  • Natural Language Processing: Allows computers to understand and generate human language.
  • Robotics: Develops intelligent robots capable of performing human-like tasks.

History of AI

AI has evolved through multiple stages:

  • 1943-1952: Early research on artificial neurons.
  • 1956: AI became an academic field.
  • 1980s: Introduction of Expert Systems.
  • 2010s-Present: Deep learning, self-driving cars, and virtual assistants.

AI Applications

  • Healthcare: AI assists in medical diagnosis.
  • Finance: AI detects fraud and automates trading.
  • E-commerce: AI personalizes shopping experiences.
  • Autonomous Vehicles: AI enables self-driving cars.

Problem Solving in AI

AI solves problems using two main approaches:

  • Uninformed Search: Methods like Breadth-First and Depth-First Search.
  • Informed Search: Heuristic-based approaches like A* Search.
